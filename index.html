Hi, I'm Manuela! I'm a bioinformatician specializing in multi-omic analysis of aging, currently completing my PhD at the University Medical Center Hamburg-Eppendorf.

About

I am completing my PhD in Medical Systems Bioinformatics at the University Medical Center Hamburg-Eppendorf, where my research focuses on transcriptomic signatures of aging in kidney and skeletal muscle tissue.

My work integrates bulk and single-nucleus RNA-seq with pathway enrichment, cell-cell communication analysis (CellChat), and cross-tissue approaches to understand how dietary and pharmacological interventions modulate aging transcriptomes.

Background: M.Sc. Bioinformatics (Universität Hamburg), B.Sc. Computer Science (Universität zu Lübeck). Previous corporate R&D experience at Beiersdorf AG.

Open to opportunities in longevity science, precision health, and translational aging research.

Research

PhD Thesis

Transcriptomic Effects of Dietary Restriction with Activin Signaling Inhibition in the ERCC1 Δ/− Aging Mouse Model

Multi-omic analysis of aging signatures across kidney and skeletal muscle tissue. 30 bulk RNA-seq samples, ~57,000 single nuclei, three experimental conditions. Methods include DESeq2, Seurat, CellChat, GSEA, and a novel rescue framework quantifying how interventions reverse age-associated gene expression changes.
